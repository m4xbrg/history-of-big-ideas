01 Domain Index

## Purpose

This note is the subject-based access point into the canon. It organizes the vault by the nine official domains so that a reader can move through the tradition by field of concern rather than by chronology alone.

The domains do not function as rigid academic departments. They are interpretive routes through the canon. Many books belong to more than one domain, and some of the strongest works matter precisely because they cross boundaries: religious texts reshape politics, literary works illuminate psychology, philosophical works alter science, and political texts reveal moral assumptions.

## How Domains Work

Each domain gathers books that speak most directly to a central field of inquiry. The purpose is not to trap a book inside one category, but to make navigation more usable.

A good way to read this note is to begin with the domain closest to your present interest, move through several linked books, and then branch outward into the related authors, concepts, and eras. If you are reading for arguments, start with philosophy, politics, economics, science, or psychology. If you are reading for civilizational formation, start with religion or history. If you are reading for imaginative depth and cultural form, start with literary classics or society and culture.

The one-line reasons below are deliberately concise. They are meant to tell you why a book belongs in a domain and what kind of doorway it offers into the broader canon.

## Religion & Worldviews

- [[002 - Hebrew Bible]] — foundational scriptural corpus for covenant, law, prophecy, creation, justice, and historical identity.
- [[003 - The Upanishads]] — central meditations on ultimate reality, selfhood, and liberation in the Indian tradition.
- [[004 - The Bhagavad Gita]] — unites duty, devotion, action, and metaphysical insight in a durable religious-philosophical synthesis.
- [[005 - The Analects]] — presents an ethical and civilizational worldview centered on ritual, learning, and moral cultivation.
- [[006 - Tao Te Ching]] — articulates a metaphysical and spiritual vision of the Dao, non-coercion, and simplicity.
- [[007 - Zhuangzi]] — radicalizes Daoist reflection through skepticism about fixed distinctions and confidence in spontaneity.
- [[020 - The New Testament]] — foundational Christian text on salvation, community, sacrifice, love, and the remaking of moral life.
- [[021 - The Qur'an]] — major scriptural text of Islam on revelation, justice, worship, law, and divine sovereignty.
- [[022 - The Dhammapada]] — concise Buddhist teaching on suffering, discipline, impermanence, and liberation.
- [[023 - Confessions]] — turns inward religiously and philosophically to explore sin, memory, grace, and conversion.
- [[024 - The City of God]] — reframes history, politics, and human destiny within a Christian theological horizon.
- [[026 - The Rule of St. Benedict]] — gives institutional form to Christian discipline, obedience, labor, and communal order.
- [[027 - The Divine Comedy]] — renders the moral and spiritual universe as a vast poetic journey through sin, purification, and beatitude.
- [[029 - Summa Theologiae]] — monumental scholastic synthesis of faith and reason, law, virtue, creation, and divine order.
- [[030 - Guide for the Perplexed]] — explores how revelation and philosophy can coexist within a monotheistic intellectual framework.
- [[100 - Man's Search for Meaning]] — asks how meaning can survive suffering, guilt, and mortality in a shattered modern world.

## Philosophy & Ethics

- [[013 - The Republic]] — classic inquiry into justice, education, the soul, and the relation between truth and political order.
- [[014 - Apology]] — dramatizes philosophy as moral vocation, public risk, and relentless questioning.
- [[015 - Nicomachean Ethics]] — foundational account of virtue, character, flourishing, and practical judgment.
- [[017 - Poetics]] — studies art, imitation, tragedy, and the structure of human emotional understanding.
- [[018 - On the Nature of Things]] — presents a materialist vision of nature, mortality, and freedom from superstition.
- [[019 - Meditations]] — Stoic reflection on self-command, transience, duty, and inner freedom.
- [[025 - The Consolation of Philosophy]] — joins classical philosophy to the experience of fortune, loss, and endurance.
- [[037 - Essays]] — makes reflective self-examination a method of philosophical and moral inquiry.
- [[040 - Discourse on Method]] — proposes disciplined doubt and methodical reasoning as paths to certainty.
- [[041 - Meditations on First Philosophy]] — reconstructs knowledge from radical doubt and secures the modern thinking subject.
- [[044 - An Essay Concerning Human Understanding]] — examines the limits, sources, and structure of human knowledge.
- [[045 - Ethics]] — offers a rational system of substance, freedom, necessity, emotion, and blessedness.
- [[048 - A Treatise of Human Nature]] — attempts a science of man that grounds morality, reason, and passion in experience.
- [[049 - Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding]] — refines skepticism about causation, certainty, and the reach of reason.
- [[056 - Critique of Pure Reason]] — redraws the conditions and limits of human cognition.
- [[057 - Groundwork of the Metaphysics of Morals]] — grounds morality in autonomy, duty, and the categorical imperative.
- [[066 - On Liberty]] — defends individuality, freedom of thought, and the moral importance of dissent.
- [[067 - Utilitarianism]] — presents one of the modern world’s most influential consequentialist ethical frameworks.
- [[085 - Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus]] — ties logic, language, and the limits of meaningful statement together in a severe philosophical architecture.
- [[087 - The Myth of Sisyphus]] — confronts absurdity and asks what philosophical honesty requires.
- [[099 - A Theory of Justice]] — reconstructs liberal political morality through fairness, equality, and rational choice.

## Politics & Power

- [[008 - The Art of War]] — treats conflict, strategy, calculation, and political judgment under conditions of danger.
- [[012 - History of the Peloponnesian War]] — reveals power politics, civic fragility, and the moral costs of imperial ambition.
- [[016 - Politics]] — analyzes constitutions, citizenship, regime types, and the purposes of political community.
- [[024 - The City of God]] — contrasts earthly power with transcendent order and redefines political ambition.
- [[031 - The Muqaddimah]] — studies dynastic rise and decline, social cohesion, and the logic of political formation.
- [[034 - The Prince]] — examines rule under conditions of instability, necessity, and force.
- [[035 - Discourses on Livy]] — turns from princely rule to republican strength, civic virtue, and institutional durability.
- [[036 - Utopia]] — uses imagined order to criticize the political and social world of its own time.
- [[042 - Leviathan]] — grounds sovereignty in fear, security, and the problem of disorder.
- [[043 - Two Treatises of Government]] — defends rights, consent, and limited government against absolutism.
- [[047 - The Spirit of the Laws]] — studies how law, climate, custom, and institutions shape political orders.
- [[051 - The Social Contract]] — asks how freedom and political authority can be reconciled.
- [[054 - Common Sense]] — transforms political argument into revolutionary clarity and public mobilization.
- [[055 - The Federalist Papers]] — theorizes constitutional design, faction, representation, and republican stability.
- [[058 - Rights of Man]] — argues for political equality and the legitimacy of democratic transformation.
- [[062 - Democracy in America]] — examines democracy as both social condition and political form.
- [[064 - The Communist Manifesto]] — interprets politics through class conflict and historical struggle.
- [[089 - The Road to Serfdom]] — warns that centralized control can erode liberty and democratic order.
- [[091 - The Open Society and Its Enemies]] — defends critical pluralism against historicism and closed political systems.
- [[092 - The Human Condition]] — studies action, plurality, labor, and the public realm.
- [[093 - The Origins of Totalitarianism]] — analyzes the ideological and institutional machinery of modern domination.
- [[094 - 1984]] — imagines the total politicization of language, memory, and human life.

## Economics

- [[031 - The Muqaddimah]] — links taxation, labor, luxury, state formation, and the material basis of civilizational life.
- [[052 - The Wealth of Nations]] — foundational text on markets, division of labor, and political economy.
- [[053 - The Theory of Moral Sentiments]] — shows that economic life rests on moral psychology and social sympathy.
- [[059 - The Interesting Narrative of the Life of Olaudah Equiano]] — exposes the human and commercial structures of Atlantic slavery.
- [[062 - Democracy in America]] — observes the social and economic conditions that support democratic equality.
- [[064 - The Communist Manifesto]] — interprets modern society through the revolutionary force of capitalism.
- [[065 - Capital, Vol. 1]] — systematic critique of commodity production, labor exploitation, and capital accumulation.
- [[089 - The Road to Serfdom]] — defends market coordination against coercive economic planning.
- [[090 - The General Theory of Employment, Interest and Money]] — reshapes macroeconomic thought around instability, demand, and state action.

## Science & Knowledge

- [[011 - Histories]] — early effort to preserve inquiry through evidence, testimony, and explanation.
- [[038 - Novum Organum]] — reorients knowledge toward experiment, induction, and methodical observation.
- [[040 - Discourse on Method]] — makes procedural reason a central feature of modern inquiry.
- [[044 - An Essay Concerning Human Understanding]] — analyzes how ideas arise and what knowledge can claim.
- [[046 - Principia Mathematica]] — monumental statement of mathematical physics and lawful natural order.
- [[048 - A Treatise of Human Nature]] — proposes that the human mind itself can be studied scientifically.
- [[049 - Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding]] — sharpens the problem of induction and empirical justification.
- [[056 - Critique of Pure Reason]] — defines the conditions that make knowledge possible and the limits that restrain it.
- [[063 - On the Origin of Species]] — transforms biological explanation through natural selection.
- [[079 - The Principles of Psychology]] — applies disciplined observation and conceptual analysis to mental life.
- [[096 - The Structure of Scientific Revolutions]] — argues that science changes through paradigm shifts, not simple accumulation.
- [[097 - Silent Spring]] — shows that scientific knowledge has public, ecological, and moral consequences.

## Psychology & Mind

- [[018 - On the Nature of Things]] — explains fear and disturbance through a naturalistic account of mind and mortality.
- [[019 - Meditations]] — develops psychological discipline through Stoic attention and self-governance.
- [[023 - Confessions]] — pioneers interior analysis of memory, desire, guilt, and transformation.
- [[037 - Essays]] — uses self-observation as a philosophical instrument.
- [[071 - The Brothers Karamazov]] — explores conscience, freedom, faith, rebellion, and divided inward life.
- [[072 - Crime and Punishment]] — studies guilt, rationalization, and the breakdown of moral self-command.
- [[078 - The Interpretation of Dreams]] — inaugurates psychoanalytic reading of desire, repression, and symbolic life.
- [[079 - The Principles of Psychology]] — major modern study of consciousness, habit, attention, and selfhood.
- [[081 - The Trial]] — portrays psychological disorientation under opaque systems of judgment.
- [[084 - Being and Time]] — analyzes anxiety, temporality, care, and existence at the deepest level.
- [[087 - The Myth of Sisyphus]] — examines the mind confronted with absurdity.
- [[100 - Man's Search for Meaning]] — interprets endurance, responsibility, and psychological survival through meaning.

## Society & Culture

- [[005 - The Analects]] — ties moral order to ritual, education, family, and the cultivation of social forms.
- [[026 - The Rule of St. Benedict]] — provides a durable model of communal discipline and ordered daily life.
- [[028 - The Tale of Genji]] — reveals courtly culture, gendered life, impermanence, and social performance.
- [[032 - The Canterbury Tales]] — displays a cross-section of social types, voices, and moral worlds.
- [[050 - Candide]] — satirizes social cruelty, philosophical complacency, and cultural self-deception.
- [[059 - The Interesting Narrative of the Life of Olaudah Equiano]] — connects slavery, commerce, race, and personhood.
- [[062 - Democracy in America]] — studies democratic habits, associations, and soft forms of conformity.
- [[075 - Madame Bovary]] — probes desire, consumption, boredom, and the cultural imagination of modern life.
- [[080 - The Souls of Black Folk]] — foundational meditation on race, double consciousness, and social exclusion.
- [[086 - The Second Sex]] — transforms the analysis of gender, embodiment, and social formation.
- [[092 - The Human Condition]] — studies labor, work, action, and the structures of the shared world.
- [[095 - Brave New World]] — imagines a culture stabilized through consumption, conditioning, and engineered desire.
- [[098 - Discipline and Punish]] — analyzes how institutions produce compliant bodies and modern forms of social control.

## Literary Classics

- [[001 - Epic of Gilgamesh]] — epic meditation on kingship, friendship, mortality, and civilizational memory.
- [[009 - The Iliad]] — presents honor, wrath, fate, and the tragic cost of heroic violence.
- [[010 - The Odyssey]] — explores homecoming, identity, cunning, and the testing of human character.
- [[027 - The Divine Comedy]] — fuses theology, poetry, ethics, and historical imagination at civilizational scale.
- [[028 - The Tale of Genji]] — offers profound literary insight into courtly life, emotion, and impermanence.
- [[032 - The Canterbury Tales]] — uses narrative variety to stage social, moral, and comic plurality.
- [[033 - Beowulf]] — joins heroism, mortality, and memory in an early medieval poetic world.
- [[039 - Don Quixote]] — reflects on fiction, illusion, dignity, and the collision of worlds.
- [[060 - Faust]] — dramatizes striving, knowledge, desire, and the modern restless self.
- [[071 - The Brothers Karamazov]] — philosophical novel of faith, doubt, guilt, and moral freedom.
- [[072 - Crime and Punishment]] — novel of transgression, suffering, and spiritual crisis.
- [[073 - War and Peace]] — fuses history, society, and inward development on an epic scale.
- [[074 - Anna Karenina]] — examines love, social judgment, family, and moral fragmentation.
- [[075 - Madame Bovary]] — depicts desire, fantasy, and the disappointments of bourgeois culture.
- [[076 - Leaves of Grass]] — poetic declaration of democratic selfhood and expansive human possibility.
- [[081 - The Trial]] — modern parable of accusation, opacity, and powerless subjectivity.
- [[082 - In Search of Lost Time]] — transforms memory and perception into a vast literary-philosophical investigation.
- [[083 - Ulysses]] — renders ordinary consciousness, city life, and formal experimentation at extreme intensity.
- [[088 - The Stranger]] — austere narrative of estrangement, contingency, and moral interpretation.
- [[094 - 1984]] — dystopian fiction of truth control, political terror, and inner resistance.
- [[095 - Brave New World]] — dystopian fiction of comfort, conditioning, and the management of humanity.

## History & Civilization

- [[001 - Epic of Gilgamesh]] — preserves an early image of city, kingship, and the problem of civilizational mortality.
- [[011 - Histories]] — foundational work of historical inquiry into peoples, conflict, and memory.
- [[012 - History of the Peloponnesian War]] — studies civilizational crisis through war, plague, rhetoric, and ambition.
- [[024 - The City of God]] — situates empire and historical time within a theological account of civilization.
- [[031 - The Muqaddimah]] — major theory of social cohesion, state development, and historical cycles.
- [[047 - The Spirit of the Laws]] — compares institutions and peoples across different historical conditions.
- [[055 - The Federalist Papers]] — records the institutional reasoning behind a modern republic.
- [[062 - Democracy in America]] — examines a society that may define the future of modern civilization.
- [[073 - War and Peace]] — reflects on history, agency, and the limits of great-man explanation.
- [[080 - The Souls of Black Folk]] — interprets the historical making of race and nation through lived fracture.
- [[093 - The Origins of Totalitarianism]] — traces the modern political conditions that made radical domination possible.
- [[097 - Silent Spring]] — widens civilizational history to include the ecological consequences of modern power.
