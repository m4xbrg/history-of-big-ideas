# Map - Book to Concept Crosswalk

## Purpose

This note is a practical navigation tool. It links the books of the canon to the concepts they most strongly illuminate, and links each concept back to the books that most centrally develop it. Its aim is not to mention every possible overlap, but to identify the most intellectually useful associations.

The crosswalk helps answer two kinds of question. First: “I am reading this book; which major ideas should I pay special attention to?” Second: “I want to study this concept; which books form the strongest route through the canon?”

## How to Use This Crosswalk

In the **By Book** section, each entry lists the strongest concepts for that book. These are not exhaustive tags. They are the ideas most central to why the book matters in the vault.

In the **By Concept** section, each concept gathers a small cluster of especially important books. These lists are intentionally selective. They are designed for navigation, comparison, and reading sequence construction rather than encyclopedic completeness.
